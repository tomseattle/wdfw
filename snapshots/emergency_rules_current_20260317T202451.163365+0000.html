Emergency fishing and shellfishing rules

Image
Photo by Alex Biswas

These updates reflect changes to the Washington Sport Fishing Rules pamphlet. Rules found in the Sport Fishing Rules pamphlet are in effect unless otherwise noted in the changes listed below.

This list shows all emergency rules currently in effect through Fishing Rule Changes. To see rules in effect on a specific date or range of dates, visit the advanced search page.

To narrow results down by water body, marine area, county, or other criteria, change the Show rules affecting dropdown from All fishing to Freshwater, Saltwater, or Shelfishing.

March 2026

  • Hatchery steelhead season extended on Salmon Creek (Clark County)
  • Razor clam digs approved for March 17–March 24
  • Adult salmon daily limit reduced in the Wind River

February 2026

  • The Dalles Pool opens for one additional day of sturgeon retention
  • Lower Columbia River spring Chinook seasons set
  • Bonneville Pool opens for one day of sturgeon retention

January 2026

  • Sturgeon retention closes in John Day Pool
  • 2026 Sport clam, oyster, and mussel seasons to change on nine Puget Sound beaches

December 2025

  • Queets, Quinault, central coast, and Grays Harbor rivers and tributaries: fishing rules update

November 2025

  • Willapa Bay tributaries fishing rules update
  • North Coast/Olympic Peninsula rivers and tributaries: steelhead and trout rules update
